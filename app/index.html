A Countdown to New Year

  Tweet

I've always been fascinated with clocks. Very specially analog clocks in every form: sand clocks, water clocks, sundials, dial clocks with numbers, without numbers... I don't need to say that I was amazed by Pixelbreaker's Polar Clock. I've spent hours watching it morph, including over New Year when all the rings go back to zero at the same time. This is my own twist using Protovis.

First, I made the rings represent time left instead of time past. I believe we found ourselves asking how much time we have left in the hour, day, month or year, more often than how much time has past. A countdown to New Year would be another way of describing it.

The order of the rings is inverted: seconds in the innermost ring and year in the outermost one, overlapping each other, following an idea of containment. This also minimizes the movement of the whole clock. I find clocks that move to much to be distracting. I also gave a different width to the rings, making minutes and hours to be the widest, in an attempt to make it easier to identify the time position in the current day by using the rings.

I removed the text from each ring and added the current hour and minute in the middle. I'd keep if text free, but I thought in case some actually decides to use this as a clock, it would be useful to know the exact time at a glance. Also, watch this space when a change in date, month or year happens.

Finally, a few aesthetic decisions based on personal taste. After some internal debate I decided to moved away from the dial clock standard of having the position zero at 90 degrees. Instead, the rings travel the clock from top to bottom on both sides, as if the time was leaking down. This makes it symmetric and hence more attractive to the eye.

Deciding on colours was hell given the amount of different choices. To keep it simple, I decided to limit the hue spectrum from green to red. The way each ring is born was an accidental feature that I love. You can preview this when the clock loads, but I hardly recommend checking it out in real time.


Enjoy!
